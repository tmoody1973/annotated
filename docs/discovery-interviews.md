# Annotated — discovery interviews & gap map

Companion to `personas.md`. Purpose: find the product gaps that would have the most
impact — specifically the ones that move Annotated from a *clip tool* toward a
*community platform* with Reddit-style network effects.

## How to use this

- **The guides are the real instrument.** Run each with ~5 real people who match the
  persona before betting the roadmap.
- **The simulated answers are HYPOTHESES**, not data — my synthesis of each persona,
  written in their voice to surface likely gaps now. Treat every one as "go check this,"
  not "this is true."
- **Method = Mom Test / jobs-to-be-done.** Ask about *past behavior and current hacks*,
  never "what features do you want" (you'll get faster horses). Dig for pain intensity:
  do they already pay, hack, or waste time around it? End every B2C interview on the
  **habit-loop probe** — where they discover content beyond who they follow, and what
  brings them back — because that loop is the gap between a tool and a platform.

## 1. Maya — independent podcaster

**Guide**
1. Walk me through the last time you wanted to share a moment from another show — step by step. What did you actually do?
2. How long did that take? Where did it break down?
3. Once you had the clip, where did it go, and what happened to it after you posted?
4. When you prep an episode, how do you collect guest soundbites today?
5. After you post something, what (if anything) pulls you back to check on it?
6. Where do you find clip-worthy moments — and how would a stranger find *your* clips if they don't already follow you?

**Simulated answers (hypotheses)**
- "I screen-recorded the audio while scrubbing a waveform, guessing where the line was. Took 20 minutes and the quality was rough." → *audio-clip moat (Annotated solves this)*
- "I posted it to X as a video card. It got a few likes and vanished by morning." → *posts die; no re-engagement loop*
- "For prep I keep a messy doc of timestamps I have to re-find every time." → *no clip library / collections*
- "People find my stuff only if they already follow me. There's no 'podcast nerds' room I can drop into." → *discovery is follow-only; no topics/communities*

**Gaps surfaced:** re-engagement loop (notifications), clip library, **topics/communities**.

## 2. Daniel — accountability journalist / media critic

**Guide**
1. Last time a public figure said something notable on audio/video — what did you do to capture and use it?
2. How did you cite or prove it? What were you worried about?
3. Where did your piece live, and how did people get back to the original?
4. Have you ever held off posting something because of a legal/fair-use worry?
5. After publishing a "receipt," what makes it travel — or die?
6. If you didn't follow anyone here, how would you find the receipts that matter on your beat?

**Simulated answers (hypotheses)**
- "I hand-transcribed it and screenshotted a tweet that later got deleted." → *no durable, sourced capture (Annotated's source-linked clip solves this)*
- "I worried clipping it was a gray area, so I paraphrased instead — weaker." → *fair-use posture is a real unlock, if it reads as defensible*
- "It traveled when other reporters could cite it cleanly. Mostly it died in the feed." → *no topic/community home; no ranking that surfaces the strongest receipts*
- "I'd want a 'media accountability' or beat-based room, not a random feed." → *topics/communities; voting-as-ranking*

**Gaps surfaced:** stronger attribution/embeds, **voting as ranking/discovery**, **topics/communities**, credibility/reputation.

## 3. Priya — researcher / educator

**Guide**
1. Last time you used a media clip in teaching or a paper — how did you capture and cite it?
2. What broke, or what did you worry would break, about that citation later?
3. How do you assign or share it with students/peers?
4. How do you keep and reuse clips across semesters?
5. What would make a clip trustworthy enough to cite in front of peers?
6. How would a student or colleague discover your clips without you sending a link?

**Simulated answers (hypotheses)**
- "I linked a YouTube timestamp; the video got taken down and the citation broke." → *durability / link rot concern*
- "I have no real library — I re-find everything each term." → *clip library / collections (top ask)*
- "Trust = exact quote + visible source. A transcript-anchored clip would actually be citable." → *transcript precision + attribution (Annotated's strengths) — lean in*
- "Students would need a course/topic space, not a social feed." → *topics/communities; possibly private/group spaces*

**Gaps surfaced:** **clip library/collections**, citation durability + embeds, topics/communities (incl. group/private).

## 4. Marcus — investor / analyst

**Guide**
1. Last time a founder/CEO said something revealing on a podcast — what did you do with it?
2. Where does your "track record" of calls live today? How would someone verify it?
3. What happens to a sharp take after you post it?
4. What makes you trust another analyst's track record?
5. What would bring you back here daily?
6. Without following anyone, how would you find the sharpest takes in your space?

**Simulated answers (hypotheses)**
- "I screenshotted the transcript — no audio, no permanence. My takes scatter across X." → *no sourced, permanent track-record artifact (Annotated's profile+clip solves this)*
- "Track record = scattered tweets. Nobody can audit my hit rate." → *reputation/track-record surface; voting as scored signal*
- "I'd come back if there were a tech/investing room with the best clips ranked." → *topics/communities + ranking*
- "Cold-start kills it — if my crowd isn't here, I won't post." → *cold-start / anchor content*

**Gaps surfaced:** **reputation/track-record**, **voting-as-ranking**, **topics/communities**, cold-start.

## 5. Renée — publisher account (B2B; interview the committee, not one person)

**Guide — audience/distribution champion**
1. Where does your content already get clipped/screenshotted, and what do you get back from it (traffic, nothing)?
2. What would a clip have to do to be worth your team's time to make?
3. How do you measure whether a distribution channel is working?
4. Walk me through the last platform you adopted — who had to say yes?

**Guide — rights/legal gatekeeper**
5. What's your current stance when someone excerpts your reporting? Takedown, ignore, negotiate?
6. What would a third-party clipping product have to guarantee for you to *not* see it as a threat?
7. What's the line between "fair excerpt that drives clicks" and "cannibalization"?

**Simulated answers (hypotheses)**
- (Champion) "Our work gets clipped everywhere with zero attribution and zero traffic back. A clip that *drove clicks* and proved it would be worth doing." → *referral analytics + strong attribution; verified account*
- (Champion) "Adopting anything means audience *and* legal sign off." → *buying committee; need both ROI proof and legal comfort*
- (Legal) "We mostly send takedowns. If clips were excerpt-limited, always linked back, and we could claim/manage our own, that's a partnership, not a threat." → *managed claim channel; verified ownership; excerpt caps*
- (Legal) "If the clip is so complete nobody clicks through, that's cannibalization — and a fight." → *cannibalization control: tight excerpt + prominent source CTA*

**Gaps surfaced:** **verified publisher accounts**, **referral/engagement analytics**, managed-claim channel, cannibalization controls, brand-safety/moderation.

## Cross-persona gap map

### The "build-next" shortlist (highest impact-to-effort, most network-effect leverage)

1. **Topics/communities** — the single biggest lever from tool → platform. Gives every clip a
   home, solves cold-start better than a follow feed, and is the structural thing Reddit has
   that Annotated doesn't.
2. **Voting-as-ranking + a discovery surface** — turns the ▲/▼ you already collect into a
   discovery engine (hot/top within a topic).
3. **Re-engagement notifications** — cheap, and the missing half of the daily-habit loop.
4. **Verified publisher accounts** — the strategic anchor-tenant bet; biggest payoff, biggest
   lift, needs the excerpt/attribution policy nailed first.

### Bounty caveat

The bounty rewards "cleanest and most complete execution" of the existing spec. Everything above
is growth/scale beyond it — **keep the bounty build spec-tight; this is the roadmap + pitch
narrative**, validated by the real interviews above.
